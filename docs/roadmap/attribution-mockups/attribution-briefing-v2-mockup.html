In October, MyRecruiter held 1,045 conversations on your behalf — 428 of them after your office had closed for the day. Those conversations became 142 leads delivered to your team, and absorbed ~140 staff-hours of conversation — three and a half work-weeks of coverage you never had to hire. Your best investment this month wasn't digital at all: the QR codes at your in-person events out-recruited your website, nearly two to one.
01 What happened

People reached out. Most of them got what they came for.

Of the 1,045 people who started a conversation, 612 engaged deeply — they clicked a resource, explored a program, or went past surface questions. 287 began an application, answering questions right in the conversation, and 142 finished — completing the handoff to your team. Volunteering was the most common reason people came (340 conversations), and people who asked about donating converted at the highest rate of any topic — 35%.

05 What to do next

Three moves, in order of confidence.

Double down

Put a QR code at every in-person event.

Event scans converted at 1.7× your website rate. Mint a separate code per placement so next month we can tell you which table, banner, or flyer did the work.

Why we say this: 29 leads from 136 conversations — your highest rate, on real volume.
Worth a look

Program-eligibility answers may be turning people away.

210 people asked if they qualify; only 10 became leads. Either the answer screens correctly — or it discourages people who would qualify. We can't tell from here. You can.

Why we say this: 4.8% conversion vs. 25.9% for volunteering questions.
Too early

Don't judge the newsletter links yet.

39 conversations is below the bar where we're willing to draw conclusions. Keep them running; we'll flag it the moment the data can speak.

Why we say this: we hold insights until n ≥ 50 per channel.
